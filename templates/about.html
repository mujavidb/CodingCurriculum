Mujavid

Solving hard problems can be very difficult, because the ambiguity of a great task is so overwhelming. To really understanding how to solve a hard problem, we must ask the right questions. Each good question elucidates the task and reduces both its complexity as well as its size. Computer Science is the amalgamation of these questions which clarifies problems and poses efficient and effective solutions. This is why I want to teach Computer Science. We cannot help people to solve all of their problems, however, we can give them the skills to effectively tackle them. When first learning to code, the process shouldn't be about getting your head around syntax and conventions. Rather, it is crucial to understand the concepts which underpin all languages. Computational thinking is a very structured form of problem solving which is essential for student of computer science. I wanted to focus on python as the language is very readable and not so syntax heavy. Students don't have to focus on semicolons being out of place, rather the focus is on improving problem solving by learning to write programs.
